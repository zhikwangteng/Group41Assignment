Tandoori Chicken

calories (260)

Tandoori Chicken is a popular dish in Malaysia, known for its vibrant red color and smoky, flavorful taste. The dish consists of marinated chicken that is roasted in a tandoor, a traditional clay oven, which imparts a distinct charred flavor. Often served with naan bread, rice, or a side of mint chutney, Tandoori Chicken is a favorite at Indian restaurants and Mamak stalls across the country.

History of Tandoori Chicken

Tandoori Chicken has its origins in the Indian subcontinent, specifically in the Punjab region. The dish became widely popular in the mid-20th century when it was introduced to a broader audience by Kundan Lal Gujral, the owner of the famous Moti Mahal restaurant in Delhi. The use of the tandoor oven, a cylindrical clay oven, is central to the dishâ€™s preparation, giving the chicken its signature smoky flavor and tender texture. As Indian cuisine spread around the world, Tandoori Chicken became a staple in many countries, including Malaysia, where it was embraced by the Indian Muslim community and integrated into local food culture. Today, it is a common offering at Mamak stalls and Indian eateries, loved for its rich, spicy flavor.



Ingredient of Tandoori Chicken

The key to Tandoori Chicken lies in its marinade, which is typically made from yogurt mixed with a blend of spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, garam masala, and chili powder. The yogurt helps to tenderize the chicken while the spices impart a deep, complex flavor. The vibrant red color, often associated with Tandoori Chicken, traditionally comes from the use of Kashmiri red chili powder or, in some cases, food coloring. After marinating for several hours or overnight, the chicken is skewered and roasted in the tandoor at high temperatures, which cooks it quickly while maintaining its juiciness. The result is a dish that is crispy on the outside, tender on the inside, and bursting with flavor. Tandoori Chicken is often garnished with lemon wedges and served with naan, rice, or a side of fresh salad, making it a well-rounded and satisfying meal.

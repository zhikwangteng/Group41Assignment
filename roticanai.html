Roti Canai

calories (300)

Roti Canai is a popular flatbread in Malaysia, known for its flaky layers and slightly crispy exterior. Often served with a side of dhal (lentil curry) or other curries, Roti Canai is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed at any time of the day, whether for breakfast, lunch, or as a snack. Its unique texture and rich flavor make it a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

History of Roti Canai

Roti Canai is a popular flatbread in Malaysia, known for its flaky layers and slightly crispy exterior. Often served with a side of dhal (lentil curry) or other curries, Roti Canai is a versatile dish that can be enjoyed at any time of the day, whether for breakfast, lunch, or as a snack. Its unique texture and rich flavor make it a favorite among locals and visitors alike.



Ingredient of Roti Canai

The basic ingredients for Roti Canai are simple yet essential to achieving its distinctive texture. The dough is made from flour, water, salt, and a generous amount of ghee or oil. Some variations may include a bit of condensed milk or sugar for a slightly sweet taste. The dough is kneaded until smooth, then allowed to rest before being stretched and flipped in the air, creating thin layers. These layers are then folded into a ball and flattened on a griddle until golden brown and crispy. The result is a flatbread that is soft and fluffy on the inside, with a slight crunch on the outside. Roti Canai is typically served with dhal curry, chicken curry, or even a side of sugar for those who prefer a sweet touch. Its adaptability and delicious taste have made it a beloved dish throughout Malaysia.

Thosai

calories (160)

Thosai, also known as dosa, is a popular South Indian dish that has become a staple in Malaysian cuisine. This thin, crispy crepe is made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black lentils) and is typically served with a variety of chutneys and sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew). Thosai is enjoyed as a breakfast dish or a light meal, beloved for its crisp texture and tangy flavor.

History of Thosai

Thosai has its origins in South India, where it has been a traditional dish for centuries. The dish made its way to Malaysia with Indian immigrants, particularly the Tamil c ommunity, who brought their culinary traditions with them. Over time, Thosai became widely popular in Malaysia, especially in Indian restaurants and Mamak stalls. Its simplicity, combined with the versatility of accompaniments, has made it a favorite among Malaysians of all backgrounds. The fermentation process not only gives Thosai its unique flavor but also makes it light and easy to digest, contributing to its widespread appeal..



Ingredient of Thosai

The main ingredients for Thosai are rice and urad dal, which are soaked separately, then ground into a smooth batter. The batter is left to ferment overnight, allowing it to develop a slightly sour taste. Once fermented, the batter is spread thinly on a hot griddle and cooked until golden brown and crispy. The result is a thin, crepe-like bread with a slightly tangy flavor. Thosai is traditionally served with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and sambar, each adding different flavors that complement the dish. Variations include masala thosai, where the crepe is filled with a spiced potato mixture, adding an extra layer of flavor and texture to this beloved dish.

Satay

calories (50)

Satay is a popular and iconic dish in Malaysia, consisting of skewered and grilled meat served with a rich peanut sauce. Known for its smoky flavor and tender texture, satay is a beloved street food enjoyed by people of all ages. It is commonly made with chicken or beef, though variations with lamb, goat, or even tofu exist.

History of Satay

Satay has its roots in Indonesia but has become a staple in Malaysian cuisine, especially among the Malay community. The dish is believed to have been influenced by Middle Eastern kebabs, brought to Southeast Asia by Arab traders. Over time, satay was adapted to local tastes, incorporating regional spices and cooking methods. It gained popularity across Malaysia as an affordable and flavorful meal that could be easily prepared and consumed on the go. Today, satay is often associated with festive occasions and gatherings, where it is enjoyed as a communal dish.



Ingredient of Satay

The key to a delicious satay lies in its marinade, which typically includes a blend of turmeric, coriander, cumin, lemongrass, garlic, and shallots, giving the meat its distinct flavor. After marinating, the meat is skewered on bamboo sticks and grilled over an open flame until it achieves a smoky char. The accompanying peanut sauce is made from ground peanuts, coconut milk, tamarind paste, and a touch of sugar, creating a creamy and slightly sweet dip that complements the savory meat. Satay is often served with sides like rice cakes (ketupat), cucumber slices, and raw onion, adding freshness and balance to the dish.

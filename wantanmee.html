WanTan Mee

calories (290)

Wantan Mee is a popular Malaysian dish that combines the tantalizing flavours of Chinese and Malaysian cuisines. With its aromatic broth, springy noodles, succulent char siu, and delectable dumplings, Wantan Mee has become a beloved staple across the country. This article dives into the origins, ingredients, preparation, and unique characteristics of this mouth-watering Malaysian delight.

The History of WanTan Mee

Wantan Mee has its roots in Chinese cuisine, specifically the Guangdong province of southern China. It was brought to Malaysia by early Chinese immigrants and evolved over time to incorporate local influences. Today, it is considered a quintessential Malaysian dish, commonly found in hawker stalls and restaurants throughout the country.

WanTan Mee in Malaysia

Malaysia offers different versions of the dish, with different states having different versions of the dish and there are versions from Johor, Pahang, Perak, Penang, Sarawak, and Selangor. The Malaysian version differs from the original in having slices of char siu added to the dish, as well as the possibility of the soup and wontons being in separate bowls, the noodles being served relatively dry, dressed with oyster sauce and garnished with chopped spring onions. Some stalls include deep-fried wontons in the dry versions as well.
